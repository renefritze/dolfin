
This demo illustrates:

* what the built in meshes in dolfin looks like



Problem definition
------------------

We do not have any equation in this demo. The demo focuses purely on the built-in meshes. We will look at the meshes:

* :py:class:`UnitIntervalMesh <dolfin.cpp.mesh.UnitIntervalMesh>`
* :py:class:`UnitSquareMesh <dolfin.cpp.mesh.UnitSquareMesh>`
* :py:class:`UnitSquareMesh <dolfin.cpp.mesh.UnitSquareMesh>`  with diagonals to the left
* :py:class:`UnitSquareMesh <dolfin.cpp.mesh.UnitSquareMesh>`  with diagonals that crosses
* :py:class:`UnitSquareMesh <dolfin.cpp.mesh.UnitSquareMesh>`  with diagonals to both left and right
* :py:class:`RectangleMesh <dolfin.cpp.mesh.RectangleMesh>`  
* :py:class:`RectangleMesh <dolfin.cpp.mesh.RectangleMesh>`   with diagonals to both left and right
* :py:class:`UnitCircleMesh <dolfin.cpp.mesh.UnitCircleMesh>`  
* :py:class:`UnitCircleMesh <dolfin.cpp.mesh.UnitCircleMesh>`  
* :py:class:`UnitCubeMesh <dolfin.cpp.mesh.UnitCubeMesh>` 
* :py:class:`BoxMesh <dolfin.cpp.mesh.BoxMesh>`
